# (20130903) EOS Aura and Shijian (SJ)-11-02 satellite conjunction [ppt (saved)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/archive/nasa/casi.ntrs.nasa.gov/20150010220.pdf) [web](https://satellitesafety.gsfc.nasa.gov/case_histories.html)

On September 3, 2013, predicted close approach

Second in a series of SJ-11 satellites launched by China into an orbit very similar to that of the Morning and Afternoon Constellations.

Aura flight controllers prepared a RMM to avoid the close approach. They did not know whether SJ-11-02 was capable of maneuvering.

A request was sent through the US State Department to its Chinese counterpart to let their space agency know of NASA's planned maneuver.
A request was sent through the US State Department to its counterpart in China to let their space agency know of NASA's planned maneuver, but there was no direct two-way coordination.

Both satellites maneuvered within hours of each other. Fortunately, the 2 maneuvers mitigated the risk.

Example of the need to improve communication with non-constellation satellites.
